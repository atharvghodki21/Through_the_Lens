Chasing Light and Beyond

"Not every journey begins with a destination. Some begin with a patch of sunlight on a distant hillside, a lamp glowing in a temple corridor, or the last golden rays slipping through the clouds before evening arrives."

These photographs were not made during a single trip, nor do they belong to one place. They are fragments collected over years of wandering — moments where light transformed something ordinary into something worth remembering.

Sometimes I found it in the quiet glow of a diya. Sometimes in the first light touching a mountain ridge. Sometimes in the silhouettes of people, monuments, and places that seemed to carry their own light long after sunset.

"This collection is not about light itself. It is about the way light changes what we see, and sometimes, how we see it."
Behind the Shot

Frames made in extreme cold

01

Key Monastery at −16°C

"I had walked 3km in the dark to be at Key Monastery before first light. The camera battery died 4 minutes after I arrived. I switched to the one from my jacket pocket, fingers working through gloves. By the time the light hit the monastery walls, I had exactly nine frames before that battery also died. This is frame seven."

Location
Key Monastery, 4,166m
Lens
24mm f/1.4
Exposure
1/80s · f/2 · ISO 1600
Temperature
−16°C at 6:08 AM
02

The River That Forgot How to Move

"The Spiti River was completely frozen for a 40-kilometre stretch. I walked onto the ice to compose this frame — which in retrospect was extremely unwise. The ice was groaning. I heard it crack somewhere downstream. I made three exposures and walked back very slowly. The light lasted another 25 minutes. I watched it from the bank."

Location
Spiti River, near Kaza
Lens
50mm f/1.2
Exposure
1/400s · f/4 · ISO 200
Note
Do not walk on frozen rivers.
"The cold does not care about your schedule. It does not negotiate. In Spiti, you either accept the terms the mountain sets, or you go home." Kaza, Spiti Valley · January 2024
