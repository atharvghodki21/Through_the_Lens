Journey Narrative

When the hills drink

"these treks started before sunrise, under rain so heavy I couldn't see twenty metres ahead. The trail to Malshej Ghat had turned into a stream. My boots were soaked by kilometre one. But I kept walking."

The Sahyadris in monsoon are not the mountains you see in travel photographs taken in winter. In July, they are alive in a way that feels almost violent — waterfalls appear on ridges that were bare stone in April, every hollow fills with cloud, and the green is so saturated it seems unreal, like a colour-graded film.

"The landscape doesn't ask permission before it becomes beautiful. You either show up, or you miss it."

This collection is not about the dramatic peaks. It is about the in-between: the mist in atmosphere at 6am, the way a single shaft of light can make a waterfall look like it is falling from the clouds themselves, the muddy silence of a trail that disappears into forest.

01

Monsoon Cathedral

"Most of the climb was spent under a grey monsoon sky, with the valley hidden behind layers of cloud. The sound of the waterfall reached us long before we could see it. As the mist began to shift, the cliffs slowly emerged from the clouds and the entire landscape opened at once. What had felt like a narrow trail moments earlier suddenly revealed itself as something vast. For a few minutes, the waterfall, the valley, and the storm above seemed to exist in perfect balance."

02

Lost In The Clouds

"Monsoon in the Sahyadris often hides more than it reveals. That morning, the valley had vanished completely beneath a moving wall of cloud. Every few seconds the mist shifted, exposing a ridge, swallowing it again, and then opening a different corner of the landscape. I waited beside the trail as the clouds drifted through the valley below. For a brief moment, the layers aligned — the ridge emerged, the mist softened, and the mountains seemed to float between earth and sky."
